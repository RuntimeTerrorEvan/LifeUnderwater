The Victims and How They're Affected

Hector's Dolphin

With a population of merely 7000, they are the smallest and rarest species of marine dolphins in the world.

Threats

Bycatch -- Since they live close to the shore, Hector's Dolphins often get accidentally caught during commercial fishing through gill and trawl nets. Gillnets are particularly dangerous, because they are made up of a fine mesh that is undectable underwater. Other Threats -- Being struck by boats, pollution and seabed mining.

Solutions

New Zealand, their country of origin, agreed to ban gilnets in a part of the Maui Dolphin's (a subspecies of Hector's Dolphin) habitat.

Narwhals

They are often reffered to as the "Unicorn of the Sea" because of their tusk. They can weight up to 4500 pounds and can grow as long as 17 feet.

Threats

Oil and Gas Development -- More development results in more shipping vessels. Such vessels create more opporotunities for collisions, as well as produce underwater noise that can interfere with the Narwhals' inter-species communication. Other Threats -- Climate Change.

Whale Sharks

Whale sharks are the largest species of shark on Earth, and the largest fish alive today!

Threats

Whale sharks are highly valued on international markets. They are also victims of bycatch and whale shark tourism.

Hawksbill Turtle s

They are named for their narrow, pointed beak, similar to that of a hawk.

Threats

They are most threatened by wildlife trade, though other dangers include loss of nesting and feeding habitats, excessive egg collection, fishery-related mortaliy, pollution and coastal development.
